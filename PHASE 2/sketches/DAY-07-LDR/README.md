# Day 07 – Analog Readings & Voltage Dividers (LDR)

## Objective

This experiment demonstrates how to use an LDR (Light Dependent Resistor) with Arduino Uno to measure light intensity through a voltage divider circuit. The analog voltage generated by the divider is converted into a digital value using the Arduino ADC and classified into different light levels.

---

## Components Used

* Arduino Uno
* LDR (Photoresistor)
* 10 kΩ Resistor
* Breadboard
* Jumper Wires
* USB Cable

---

## Circuit Description

The LDR and a 10 kΩ resistor are connected as a voltage divider.

Connection:

* LDR Leg 1 → 5V
* LDR Leg 2 → A0
* 10 kΩ Resistor Leg 1 → A0
* 10 kΩ Resistor Leg 2 → GND

Circuit:

5V
|
|
LDR
|
|-------> A0
|
10kΩ
|
|
GND

---

## Why a 10 kΩ Resistor?

A 10 kΩ resistor is commonly used with LDRs because:

* It provides a good voltage range for the ADC.
* It prevents saturation at most indoor lighting levels.
* It allows clear distinction between dark and bright conditions.
* Current consumption remains very low.

---

## ADC Information

Arduino Uno ADC Resolution:

* 10-bit ADC
* Range: 0 – 1023
* Reference Voltage: 5V

Voltage Calculation:

Voltage = Raw_ADC × (5.0 / 1023)

## Calibration Results

### Test 1 – Dark Condition

LDR completely covered.

Raw ADC Value = 0

### Test 2 – Room Light

LDR exposed to normal room lighting.

Raw ADC Value = 818

### Test 3 – Phone Flashlight

Phone flashlight held approximately 5 cm from the LDR.

Raw ADC Value = 986

## Observations

* ADC value increases as light intensity increases.
* Covering the LDR decreases the voltage at A0.
* Flashlight exposure produces near-maximum ADC readings.
* Averaging multiple samples significantly reduces noise and jitter.

---

## Conclusion

The LDR voltage divider was successfully implemented and calibrated. Analog readings were obtained from the Arduino ADC, converted into voltage values, and categorized into DARK, AMBIENT, and BRIGHT light conditions. The calibration values can be reused in future projects involving threshold-based lighting control and environmental monitoring.
