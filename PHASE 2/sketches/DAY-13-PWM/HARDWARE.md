\### Arduino Uno



The Arduino Uno is based on the ATmega328P microcontroller and operates at 5 V. It acts as the brain of the system by receiving commands from the Serial Monitor and controlling the motor, relay, LED, and emergency stop function.



\### L298N Motor Driver Module



The L298N is a dual H-bridge motor driver capable of operating motors from 5 V to 35 V. It receives control signals from the Arduino and provides sufficient current to drive the DC motor. It also allows speed control using PWM and direction control using digital signals.



\### DC Motor



The DC motor converts electrical energy into mechanical motion. In this project, the motor speed is controlled by varying the PWM signal generated by the Arduino through the L298N motor driver.



\### Relay Module



The relay module is an electrically operated switch that works at 5 V. It isolates the low-power Arduino circuit from the higher-power valve circuit and allows the Arduino to turn the solenoid valve ON and OFF safely.



\### Solenoid Valve



The solenoid valve is an electromechanical device used to control the flow of liquid. When the relay is energized, the valve opens and allows fluid to pass through. When the relay is de-energized, the valve closes and stops the flow.



\### Emergency Stop Push Button



The emergency stop push button is used as a safety feature. When pressed, it triggers an interrupt that immediately stops the motor and closes the valve. The system remains in the emergency state until it is reset.



\### LED



The LED serves as a status indicator. It blinks while the motor is running and stays ON(without blinking) when the motor stops, providing a visual indication of the system's operation.



\### Power Supply



The power supply provides the required electrical energy to the system. The Arduino and relay module operate at 5 V, while the motor and solenoid valve are powered using an external supply suitable for their voltage ratings.
